SAFETY NOTICE

Prescription opioids must not be used by female patients who are pregnant. There is a higher risk that the unborn baby can develop neonatal abstinence syndrome if the mother is pregnancy while taking any prescription opioid in any amount, even for a short period of time. There is also a greater risk for miscarriage and low birth weight. Misuse and/or abuse of prescription opioids puts patients at risk to developing an addiction to opioids, which can lead to overdose death if the addiction is not treated. Because of the risks associated with prescription opioids, prescription opioids can only be marketed under a special restricted distribution program. This program is called OpiACT!®. Under this program, prescribers must be registered and activated with the OpiACT! ® Program and can prescribe prescription opioids only to registered patients who meet all the requirements of OpiACT! ®.
Please refer to the prescription opioids package inserts for full prescribing and dispensing instructions.
